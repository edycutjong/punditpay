One loop, extreme depth

Form a pick on-device → sign an x402 USD₮ tip over HTTP → enforce the Transaction-Policy cap. Everything serves that moment.

01 · QVAC

Reason on-device

A local GGUF model with tool-calling watches the match. No cloud, no API key — your judgment stays on your machine.

02 · decide

The model calls pay_tip

When its confidence clears your rule ("only above 70%"), the model — not a button — decides to pay.

03 · policy

The wallet checks the cap

A default-deny Transaction Policy judges every payment before signing. Over the cap → PolicyViolationError.

04 · x402

Pay over plain HTTP

Discover price → sign USD₮ → receive receipt, one round-trip. The payment is the credential.

Why only QVAC + WDK

Take them out and you'd need a cloud LLM, a payment processor, an account system, a checkout, a custodial wallet, and a limits engine — six systems and a data-sharing agreement.

QVAC completion() + tools

The brain is a local file. loadModel → completion({tools}) streams reasoning and emits structured pay_tip calls — fully offline.

WDK self-custodial keys

BIP-39 seed on-device via @tetherto/wdk. The agent owns its wallet; keys never transmit — only signed payments do.

WDK Transaction Policies

A default-deny ALLOW/DENY engine wraps every signature. The cap lives in the wallet, not in a server you have to trust.

x402 over Spark

Zero-fee Spark settlement makes cent-sized tips economical. HTTP 402 → signed payment → resource, no account anywhere.

Autonomous, but bounded

The demo's favorite moment is a refusal: after 1.00 USD₮ of great football, the agent tries one more tip — and its own wallet says no, live, in red.

“I wanted to tip the streamer who called the comeback in stoppage time — but it meant an account, a card, a checkout, and handing my data to a payment processor. The moment passed. I never tipped.” — the tip that never happened, and the reason PunditPay exists
